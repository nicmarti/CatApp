The story behind this demo

You're in a train, somewhere in France, heading to Paris. Those past 2 days in la Rochelle, France, were great. Very good conference at the 2nd JUG Summer Camp. This is a good conference in the south-west of France, organized by SERLI. One of the most interesting session was an one hour live coding by David Gageot.

David and I are good friend. We're both members of the Java community for a few years. We usually talk at the same conference. This year I did a Keynote, and David wrote its own Web framework in one hour. This guy is a great developer. With an Eclipse, some SimpleHTTP, a bit of Google Guava and a lot of energy, he wrote a simple HTTP application in less than 40 minuts.

This was to demonstrate that maybe, we don't really need any web framework. Its demo was exactly what you're looking at : select the cuttiest cat, that's all. I decided to wrote a Play! Framework version of its demo, and it tooks me 3 hours. I spent a lot of time on the jQuery side, not being a Jedi master in this area.

Kudo to Guillaume Bort for this great framework, I love Play! Framework.

Nicolas Martignole, 17 septembre 2011

Quick and stupid demo for Play! Framework to demonstrate some nice simple jQuery features. High-five to @dgageot for this idea.
